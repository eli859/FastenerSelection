Torque — Select grade, diameter, and condition above

T = K·d·F (nut factor method). For 3D-printed or plastic substrates use 30–50% preload target to avoid cracking. Stainless fasteners in stainless nuts: apply anti-seize — galling can seize threads before target torque is reached. Values are nominal; verify with fastener manufacturer data for safety-critical joints.

Thread locking guide — robotics applications

Loctite 222
Low strength / removable
For small fasteners M2–M6 that need frequent adjustment or tuning. Finger-removable after cure. Ideal for sensor mounts, encoder brackets, limit switch positions.
M2–M6 removable up to 3 N·m
Loctite 243
Medium strength — most common
Workhorse for robotics. Use on all structural fasteners M4–M12 subject to vibration. Removable with hand tools. Works on mildly oily surfaces. Standard choice for drive trains, gearboxes, motor mounts.
M4–M12 hand-removable oil-tolerant
Loctite 263
High strength — semi-permanent
For joints that must never back out under shock or vibration but may need future service. Requires heat (~250°C) or significant torque to remove. Use on wheel hubs, main chassis bolts, transmission housings.
M6–M20 heat to remove shock-resistant
Loctite 271
High strength — permanent
Permanent bond. Use only on fasteners that will never be removed — press-fit studs, permanently staked hardware, set screws on precision shafts. Destroying the part is likely on removal.
permanent set screws studs
Loctite 609
Retaining compound
For press fits, bearings, and bushings in housings. Not a thread locker — fills the gap in slip-fit or clearance-fit assemblies. Use to retain bearings in aluminum housings, pulleys on shafts.
press fits bearings not thread locking
Nyloc nut
Mechanical — no adhesive
Preferred over Loctite when the joint will be assembled and disassembled repeatedly. Nylon insert provides consistent prevailing torque. Use where chemical contamination of threads is a concern (food, medical, water contact).
reusable ~5× no contamination consistent
Common mistakes
· Applying Loctite to stainless threads without wiping clean first — oil prevents cure
· Using 271 on small fasteners (M4 and under) — threads will strip on removal
· Applying to aluminum threads directly — use a primer (Loctite 7649) for best adhesion
· Torquing before Loctite reaches gel stage (~10 min) — wash-out under the nut
· Re-using Loctite-coated fasteners — clean threads with acetone and re-apply fresh
